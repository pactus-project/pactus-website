## Delegation Is Here!

April was defined by a major protocol upgrade and an open door for new
participants. Here is a recap of the month's key highlights.

## Tallinn: A Major Protocol Upgrade

The biggest news of April:
**[Pactus Blockchain Version 1.13.0 (Tallinn)](https://pactus.org/2026/04/04/pactus-1.13.0-tallinn-released/)**
was released on **April 4, 2026**.

This was not a routine maintenance release. Tallinn introduced a
protocol-level upgrade with one of the most requested features in Pactus
history: **Validator Delegation**, as specified in
**[PIP-49](https://pips.pactus.org/PIPs/pip-49)**.

Here is what that means in plain language: before Tallinn, to become a Pactus
validator, you had to own PAC tokens to stake. Now, PAC holders can delegate
their tokens to validator operators, and those operators can earn block rewards
without needing to buy tokens upfront.

- **Token holders** earn passive rewards
- **Validators** can join without the initial capital barrier
- **Everyone** benefits

**[Download Tallinn now →](https://pactus.org/download/)**

> **Important:** Tallinn is a protocol upgrade. If you are still running an
> older version, your node will no longer participate in block production and
> you will stop earning rewards. Upgrade immediately.

## A New Era for Participation

Following the activation of PIP-49, the team published a dedicated
announcement on **April 15, 2026**: Validator Delegation is now live on the
Pactus network.

To celebrate the launch and lower the barrier to entry even further, Pactus
launched a **special delegation campaign** for validators not yet part of the
network, including validators already active on other blockchains.

Here is what new participants receive through this program:

- **16,000 PAC** in delegated stake — distributed across 16 validators
  (1,000 PAC each)
- **Zero cost** — no tokens required to get started
- **50% of block rewards** — 0.5 PAC per block, paid directly to your wallet

And the infrastructure requirements? Minimal. A Pactus validator can run on
just 1–2 CPU cores and even on a shared server. If you already run validators
on other chains, adding Pactus to your stack is simple.

Getting started is three steps:

1. Set up and launch your Pactus validator
2. Share your first 16 validator addresses with the team
3. Receive your delegated stake and start earning

This is what inclusive, community-driven blockchain growth looks like.

**[Read the full announcement →](https://pactus.org/2026/04/15/validator-delegation-is-live/)**

Make it your go-to tool for tracking everything happening on-chain.
