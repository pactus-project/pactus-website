## What the L1 World Was Actually Doing in May

### Solana Replaced Its Entire Consensus Engine in Testing

On May 11, Solana developer Anza confirmed that **Alpenglow**, the network's
biggest proposed consensus overhaul to date, is live on a community test cluster,
allowing validators to test transitioning from the blockchain's current
Proof-of-History and TowerBFT system to a faster architecture ahead of a
potential mainnet rollout.

To put that in plain terms: Solana is attempting to completely remove the two
core mechanisms its entire chain was built on, and replace them with something
new. The upgrade aims to slash transaction finality from 12 seconds to 150
milliseconds. Solana co-founder Anatoly Yakovenko said at Consensus Miami 2026
on May 7 that Alpenglow could reach mainnet as soon as next quarter if testing
proceeds without issues. That is a significant "if." A mainnet launch had
originally been targeted for Q1 2026 but was pushed back as development
continued.

### Ethereum's Glamsterdam

The Ethereum Foundation confirmed the **Glamsterdam** protocol upgrade is now
expected to launch sometime in the third quarter of 2026, having originally
targeted June. The delay comes alongside a new 200-million gas limit target, a
significant jump from the current gas limit of approximately 60 million. The
Ethereum Foundation's May 2026 protocol update reported progress around
Glamsterdam devnets, ePBS work, BAL optimizations, and gas limit research. The
ambition is real, so is the complexity.

### Bitcoin Core 31.0

Bitcoin Core 31.0 shipped in May, rebuilding transaction ordering around
clusters, improving how block templates are assembled and how fee-bumping is
evaluated. Quietly significant. But a louder debate continues: **BIP-110**, a
proposed soft fork to restrict arbitrary data on the Bitcoin chain, began miner
signaling in December 2025, requiring a 55% threshold for activation, with a
maximum activation window closing around September 2026. As of early 2026, miner
signaling remains minimal and the threshold has not been approached. Bitcoin's
governance over what belongs on the chain is unresolved.

## The RWA Story: Who Is Actually Winning

The tokenized RWA market hit **$34.5 billion** in May 2026, up over 100%
year-on-year. Ethereum leads, capturing roughly 33% of the entire market. Solana
is closing in fast: it captured 97.3% of all tokenized-equity spot trading
volume in May, recording $868.9 million against just $23.6 million across all
other chains combined.

The bigger story is standardization. On May 27, **ERC-7943** — the Universal RWA
standard — reached Final status on Ethereum. It was authored by Dario Lo Buglio,
co-founder of Brickken, who built it to solve one specific problem: institutions
needed programmable compliance controls while developers were stuck rewriting
custom logic for every single RWA token. The spec is now frozen and open for
production use across all EVM chains. The Capital Markets and Technology
Association (CMTA) has already integrated it into their open-source tokenization
framework deployed in institutional initiatives globally.

On May 9, BlackRock separately filed with the SEC for two additional tokenized
fund structures. The institutional conviction is not slowing down.

## Why This Matters for Pactus

Three of the biggest chains are simultaneously wrestling with their most
significant technical overhauls in years. A consensus engine replacement on
Solana. A hard fork delay on Ethereum. A governance war on Bitcoin. These are
hard problems precisely because these chains are large, complex, and have
competing stakeholders pulling in every direction.

And the RWA wave is accelerating regardless. Institutions are not waiting for the
technology to be perfect — they are building on what is stable, auditable, and
live.

Pactus was designed for exactly this environment: lightweight, decentralized, no
hard forks, no governance wars, no hardware barriers. When enough validators
signal support for a new protocol version, the upgrade activates and the network
moves forward as a single, unified history.

That is not luck. That is the design working.
