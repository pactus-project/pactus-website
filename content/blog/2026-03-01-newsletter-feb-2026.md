## February in Review

February brought a significant update to Pactus with the release of v1.12.0
(Zürich), introducing remote node management capabilities that make running
validators more accessible than ever.

## Zürich Released: Remote Node Management

On February 21, Pactus v1.12.0 (Zürich) launched with a game-changing feature:
remote GUI node management.

You can now connect, monitor, and manage your Pactus node remotely through the
graphical interface. No more relying solely on command-line tools or SSH access.
Point your GUI at any remote node and control everything visually.

This is especially useful for validators running nodes on cloud servers or
remote hardware. You get the security and reliability of a remote setup with the
convenience of local GUI management.

**[Download Zürich](https://pactus.org/download)** if you haven't upgraded yet.

## Network Health

5,455 validators are currently securing the network according to the block
explorer - steady growth from January's ~5,200.

18.5 million+ transactions have been processed on Pactus since mainnet launch.
The network continues operating smoothly with consistent ~10 second block
times.

Zürich adoption has been smooth. No major issues reported post-release, and
validators have been upgrading steadily throughout the month.

## What Happened in Crypto

February was relatively calm in the broader crypto market, but a few things
stood out:

Regulatory developments progressed in several jurisdictions. The EU's MiCA
framework effects became more visible, and the US provided additional guidance
on digital asset classification. Clearer rules make it easier for legitimate
projects to operate.

Bitcoin network continues showing strength. Mining difficulty hit new all-time
highs again, proving that regardless of price action, the underlying
infrastructure keeps growing stronger.

Market stability was the theme. No wild pumps or catastrophic crashes - just
steady price action while developers kept building. Boring markets are often
the healthiest ones for actual technological progress.
